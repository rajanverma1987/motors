# 2.5 Lead Distribution and Lead Pricing

The platform distributes motor repair inquiries to subscribed repair shops.
Each lead can generate revenue through **shared lead pricing or exclusive lead pricing**.

---

# Lead Generation

Leads originate from:

* Local SEO pages
* Repair shop directory pages
* Emergency repair forms
* Contact forms
* Direct website searches

When a customer submits a repair request, the system creates a **Lead Record**.

Lead information includes:

Customer name
Company
Phone
Email
City / location

Motor details

Motor type
Motor HP
Voltage
Problem description
Urgency level

Optional

Motor photos

---

# Lead Qualification

Before distributing a lead, the system performs basic qualification.

Checks include:

Location detection
Motor size (HP)
Service type required
Emergency or normal repair

The system then identifies repair shops capable of handling the request.

Matching criteria:

Service area radius
Motor capability (HP or voltage limits)
Services offered (rewinding, repair, etc.)
Subscription status

Only **active subscribed repair shops** receive leads.

---

# Lead Distribution Process

1. Customer submits repair request
2. Lead is created in the system
3. System detects customer location
4. System finds nearby repair shops within service radius
5. System checks capability match
6. Lead is assigned based on pricing model

---

# Lead Pricing Models

The system supports **three pricing types**.

---

# 1. Shared Leads

Shared leads are distributed to **multiple repair shops simultaneously**.

Example:

Lead is sent to 3 repair shops.

Each shop receives the lead and can contact the customer.

Pricing example:

$25 per lead per shop

Example revenue:

1 lead × 3 shops = $75 revenue

Shared leads work well for:

Standard repair inquiries
Non-urgent requests

---

# 2. Exclusive Leads

Exclusive leads are sent to **only one repair shop**.

Only that shop can contact the customer.

Pricing example:

$80 – $120 per lead

Exclusive leads are typically used for:

Emergency repairs
Large motors
Industrial breakdowns

These leads are more valuable.

---

# 3. Emergency Leads

Emergency leads occur when a motor failure stops production.

These leads are marked as **urgent**.

Characteristics:

Immediate repair required
Customer wants fast response

Distribution rules:

Lead is sent to the **first available capable shop**.

Pricing example:

$120 – $200 per lead

These leads are usually exclusive.

---

# Lead Assignment Priority

Repair shops can have different priority levels.

Factors affecting lead assignment:

Subscription plan
Lead purchase balance
Service capability match
Distance from customer

Higher priority shops receive leads first.

---

# Lead Purchase Model

Repair shops maintain a **lead credit balance**.

Example:

Shop deposits $200
Each lead deducts $25

System deducts credits when lead is delivered.

If credit balance reaches zero, leads stop being delivered.

---

# Lead Delivery Methods

Once a lead is assigned, it is delivered through multiple channels.

Email notification
SMS alert
SaaS dashboard notification

The notification contains:

Customer contact details
Motor details
Repair request description

---

# Lead Tracking

Statuses include:

New

Converted
Lost

This helps measure lead effectiveness.

---

# Lead Conversion

When a repair shop wins the job, the lead can be converted into:

## 3.6.1 Motor Tag Printing and Mobile Status Tracking

When a motor arrives at the repair shop, the system generates a **Motor Job Tag**.
This tag is attached physically to the motor and allows technicians to quickly track and update the repair status using mobile devices.

---

### Purpose

Motor repair shops typically track motors using paper tags.
This feature digitizes that process and connects the **physical motor in the shop with the digital work order in the SaaS system**.

It allows technicians to update job status without needing to access a desktop computer.

---

### Motor Receiving Process

1. Motor arrives at the repair shop.
2. Staff creates a **Work Order** in the system.
3. The system generates a **Motor Job Tag**.
4. The tag is printed and attached to the motor.
5. The tag contains a **QR code or barcode** linked to the work order.

---

### Motor Tag Information

The printed motor tag includes:

Job Number
Customer Name
Motor HP
Serial Number
Date Received
Reported Problem

Machine-readable code

QR Code or Barcode

The QR code links directly to the **Work Order page in the SaaS system**.

---

### Technician Workflow

During the repair process:

1. Technician scans the QR code using a mobile phone.
2. The system opens the corresponding work order.
3. Technician updates job status directly from the mobile interface.

Example updates:

Inspection Complete
Rewinding Started
Reassembly Complete
Testing Started
Testing Complete
Ready for Pickup

---

### Benefits

Faster job updates on the shop floor
Technicians do not need to leave the work area
Reduces errors in job tracking
Improves visibility of job progress for managers

---

### Mobile Interface

When a QR code is scanned, the mobile screen displays:

Job number
Motor information
Current status

Action buttons for quick updates.

Example buttons:

Start Inspection
Start Rewinding
Start Testing
Mark as Ready

Each action updates the **Shop Floor Job Board automatically**.

---

### Integration with Shop Floor Board

When technicians update job status using the motor tag:

The work order automatically moves to the next column in the **Shop Floor Job Board**.

Example flow:

Received → Inspection → Rewinding → Testing → Ready

This ensures the shop board always reflects the real-time status of motors.

---

### Tag Reprinting

The system allows users to reprint tags if needed.

Reasons may include:

Tag damaged
Tag lost
Additional labels required

Users can reprint from the **Work Order screen**.

---

### Optional Future Enhancements

Automated status tracking with scanning stations

These features can further improve shop floor efficiency.


## 3.7 Billing and Invoice Creation Process

### Purpose

Generate invoices for completed repair jobs and deliver them to customers with an optional online payment link.

Invoices are created directly from the **completed Work Order and the approved Quote**.

---

# Invoice Creation Workflow

### Step 1 — Job Completion

When the repair job is finished, the **Work Order status becomes “Completed” or “Ready”**.

Staff opens the completed Work Order.

On the Work Order screen there is an action button:

Create Invoice

---

### Step 2 — Create Invoice Screen

When the user clicks **Create Invoice**, the system opens the Invoice Creation page.

The system automatically copies data from the **approved Quote** and the **Work Order**.

Copied information includes:

Customer information
Motor details
Work order number
Labor charges
Parts used from quote
Quoted repair items

This prevents re-entering data.

---

### Step 3 — Add Additional Charges

Before finalizing the invoice, staff can add additional items if necessary.

Possible additional items include:

Additional labor hours
Additional parts used
Testing charges
Pickup / delivery charges
Emergency service charges
Miscellaneous shop charges

These items are added as additional **invoice line items**.

---

### Step 4 — Final Invoice Generation

After reviewing the invoice, the user clicks:

Create Invoice

The system then:

Generates the final invoice
Assigns an invoice number
Links the invoice to the Work Order and Customer

Invoice status becomes:

Generated

---

After the invoice is created, the system automatically starts the **Invoice Delivery Process**.

## 3.9.2 Vendor Invoice Attachment and Payment Tracking

### Purpose

Allow repair shops to attach **vendor invoices** to Purchase Orders so that supplier billing and payments can be tracked accurately.

This feature ensures that all supplier invoices related to a PO are stored in the system and that the system can determine when a PO has been **fully paid or partially paid**.

---

# Vendor Invoice Attachment Workflow

### Step 1 — Open Purchase Order

Staff opens the **Purchase Order (PO) screen**.

The PO screen displays:

PO number
Vendor
PO items
PO total amount
PO status

On this screen there is an action button:

Attach Vendor Invoice

---

### Step 2 — Upload Vendor Invoice

When the user clicks **Attach Vendor Invoice**, a form appears.

The user can upload the vendor invoice document.

Supported file formats may include:

PDF
Image files (JPG, PNG)
Scanned documents

This ensures that all supplier invoices are stored with the related PO.

---

### Step 3 — Enter Vendor Invoice Details

Along with the document upload, the user enters invoice details.

Fields include:

Vendor Invoice Number
Invoice Date
Invoice Amount
Tax Amount (if applicable)
Notes

The system records the invoice as part of the PO record.

---

### Step 4 — Multiple Invoices per PO

A single Purchase Order may have **multiple vendor invoices**.

Examples:

Partial deliveries
Multiple shipments
Separate billing for parts

The system must allow attaching **multiple invoices to the same PO**.

Each invoice is stored with:

Invoice number
Invoice amount
Attachment document
Invoice date

---

# Vendor Invoice Tracking

Remaining Balance
